1. What is Pairs Trading?

配對交易 (Pair Trading) 是一種市場中性 (Market Neutral) 的投資策略，最早由華爾街投行摩根士丹利 (Morgan Stanley) 在 1980 年代開發。其核心概念是尋找歷史上價格走勢高度相關的兩檔商品（如兩檔同產業的股票，或相關性極高的期貨合約），並在它們之間的價格差距（價差 Spread）出現異常擴大時進行操作：

Core Trading Rule:
When spread widens anomaly ➡️ **Short the overvalued asset & Long the undervalued asset**, waiting for the spread to converge to its mean.

The main benefit: **No matter if the market rises or crashes, as long as the relative spread reverts to normal, you profit.** This is "market neutrality".

2. Statistical Arbitrage and Mean Reversion

Pairs trading relies on **Mean Reversion**. A linear combination of two correlated assets yields a spread series that fluctuates around a stable historical mean.

均值回歸示意圖概念

Imagine a puppy (Asset A) walked by its owner (Asset B). Though the puppy runs ahead or lags behind, the leash (correlation) pulls it back. In markets, the leash is the industrial fundamentals or arbitrage forces.

3. Asset Selection & Cointegration Test

Selecting assets requires more than basic correlation, which can diverge during market transitions. We need a mathematical guarantee of convergence.

Quant trading uses the **Cointegration Test** (like Engle-Granger two-step method):

Step 1: OLS regression:
Price_A = β * Price_B + α + Residual

Step 2: Perform ADF Test on Residuals. If residuals are Stationary, the assets are cointegrated.

Common pairs: direct competitors (e.g. KO vs PEP), supply chain partners, or highly substitutable futures (Soybeans vs Soyoil).

4. Z-Score and Trading Signals

Once cointegration is proven, we compute the **Z-Score** of the spread. It measures how many standard deviations the current spread is from the mean:

Spread = Price_A - (β * Price_B)
Z-Score = (Spread - Mean) / StdDev

Entry and Exit Signals Example:

  • **Entry Signal**: When Z-Score > +2.0 (spread is over-widened), Short A and Long B.
  • **Entry Signal**: When Z-Score < -2.0 (spread is over-narrowed), Long A and Short B.
  • **Exit Signal**: When Z-Score reverts back to 0 (mean), close both sides to take profit.

5. Risk Management & Adjustments

Though lower risk than outright trades, pairs trading is not risk-free arbitrage. The main risk is **breakdown of cointegration** (e.g., corporate scandals or regulatory black swans).

Three Pillars of Pairs Trading Risk Control:

  1. **Hard Stop**: If Z-Score exceeds ±3.0 and continues diverging, cut losses manually.
  2. **Time Stop**: If the spread does not converge within 30 days, exit as the market dynamics may have structurally changed.
  3. **Capital Allocation**: Pairs trade involves two legs; keep total position allocation under 10% of equity for a single pair.
